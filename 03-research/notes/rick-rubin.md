## Notes

- Co-founded Def Jam Recordings in the early 1980s, producing early records for the Beastie Boys, Public Enemy and Run-D.M.C.
- Later founded Def American Recordings (later American Recordings), working across genres including metal (Slayer, Danzig) and, most notably, Johnny Cash's late-career American Recordings series.
- Publicly reframed his own "produced by" credit as reduction rather than addition — his method is described as stripping a recording down until what remains "has no place to hide."
- The Johnny Cash sessions removed studio embellishment (strings, backing vocals, ornate percussion) in favour of voice with minimal instrumentation, revived Cash's career commercially and critically, and won multiple Grammy Awards.
