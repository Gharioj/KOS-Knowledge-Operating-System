# Constitutional Contribution

## Definition

Constitutional Contribution is a [Constitutional Capacity](constitutional-capacity.md) put into actual use — the point at which a recognised ability is exercised toward a function or institution's need, producing a real effect in the world. It is not the capacity itself, nor merely its recognition; it is the act of exercising it.

## Constitutional Principle

Contribution exists the moment capacity is exercised, whether or not the system subsequently records, credits or rewards it. A constitutional methodology must keep contribution as fact distinct from [Recognised Contribution](recognised-contribution.md) as record — collapsing the two is how real work goes uncounted, and how institutions come to define reality by their own bookkeeping.

## Why Contribution Must Be Kept Separate from Its Record

A mix is completed, a song is written, a set is played: the contribution happens in the act, at the moment of exercise, independent of any ledger, credit line or statement produced afterward. If contribution were treated as existing only once recognised, an institution's failure to record it would erase the underlying fact rather than merely leaving it undocumented. The constitutional order instead treats contribution as prior and real, and recognition as a subsequent, contestable act performed on it — never the other way round.

## Relationship to Constitutional Capacity

Contribution is [Constitutional Capacity](constitutional-capacity.md) exercised — capacity that has been recognised and then actually put to use, not merely held or credentialed. Recognition without exercise is standing without contribution; exercise is what turns capacity into contribution.

## Relationship to Recognised Contribution

[Recognised Contribution](recognised-contribution.md) is what a Constitutional Contribution becomes once the system records, credits or accounts for it. The two are routinely conflated because most institutions only track contribution once it is recognised — but the constitutional order treats the exercise of capacity as real the moment it happens, whether or not a record is ever produced for it.

## Examples from the Creative Ecosystem

- A producer's mix is a contribution the moment it is completed and delivered, regardless of whether the eventual credits list them.
- A manager's unpaid early-career coordination is a contribution even before any formal agreement recognises it.
- A ghostwriter's lyric is a contribution to the finished work whether or not the release credits them.

## Relationship to Technology

Technology can make contribution more visible and traceable — session logs, timestamped files, provenance tools — narrowing the gap between an act of contribution and its eventual recognition. It does not change the underlying principle: the act of contributing remains prior to, and independent of, whatever technology is later used to detect or verify it.

## Notes

This distinction matters most where labour goes uncredited or undercompensated — see [Recognised Contribution](recognised-contribution.md) when populated for how the recognition step is actually performed and where it tends to fail.
