# Mute Records

## Source

- [Mute Records — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mute_Records)

## Notes

- Founded by Daniel Miller in London, 1978, releasing "TVOD / Warm Leatherette" by his own project The Normal.
- Built its identity around Miller's own A&R judgment — Depeche Mode, Yazoo, Erasure, Nick Cave and the Bad Seeds, Moby, Goldfrapp — sustained artist relationships over decades rather than transactional signings.
- Sold to EMI in 2002 (£23m); Miller took Mute independent again in 2010, but EMI retained the archive rights.
- BMG Rights Management acquired the Mute back catalogue from Universal in December 2012, following the regulator-forced sale of EMI's European assets.
- BMG's CEO described the catalogue as "a testament to the vision and A&R skills of Daniel Miller" — the catalogue's acquisition value is explicitly attributed to one person's sustained creative judgment.
- Miller stepped back from daily operations around 2021 but retained the chairman role, still overseeing creative direction and signings.
