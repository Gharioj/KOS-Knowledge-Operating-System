# Constitutional Economics

## Definition

Constitutional Economics is how [Constitutional Value](constitutional-value.md) moves through the system once it exists — who captures it, who it passes through, and what happens when the entity carrying a [Constitutional Function](constitutional-functions.md) itself becomes something that can be bought and sold.

## Constitutional Principle

A business or institution that performs a Constitutional Function is, by virtue of performing that function, itself a potential capital asset — transactable as a going concern, bought or sold independent of whether it directly owns the creative IP its function relates to.

This is evidenced across four independent Research Phase 1 analyses — Artist Managers, Publishers & Catalogue Funds, Booking Agents & Touring Agencies, and Streaming Platforms & DSPs — spanning four different participant types, with no disconfirming instance found ([CQ-4](constitutional-analysis-method.md#cq-4-a-function-performing-entity-is-itself-a-potential-capital-asset)). It meets the accumulated-evidence criteria this methodology requires before a question can be considered for resolution. It is stated here as the strongest current account of what the evidence shows, not as a formally Resolved Constitutional Principle — that remains a distinct, dated act for the Owner, not taken by this document.

## Why an Institution's Legitimacy and Its Capital Status Are Different Things

A function-performing institution's legitimacy is earned by faithfully performing the functions it holds responsibility for ([Constitutional Institutions](constitutional-institutions.md)). Whether that same institution can be bought and sold is a separate question, governed by ownership structure, debt, and market conditions rather than by how well it performs its function. Wasserman Music's acquisition of Paradigm's live-music roster is the clearest instance: a business holding no creative IP whatsoever — only relationships and contracts — was still bought and sold as a capital asset once financial distress forced the sale ([Booking Agents & Touring Agencies](booking-agents.md)). Bandcamp shows the same pattern at the infrastructure level: creator-favourable terms are a policy choice of current ownership, not a durable property of the platform, and became uncertain the moment ownership changed twice within two years ([Streaming Platforms & DSPs](streaming-platforms.md)).

## Relationship to Constitutional Value

[Constitutional Value](constitutional-value.md) is what moves; Constitutional Economics is the description of how it moves, including through changes of ownership over the institution carrying the function that produced it.

## Relationship to Constitutional Institutions

An institution's economic status as a transactable asset does not depend on its legitimacy, and its legitimacy does not depend on who currently owns it ([Constitutional Institutions](constitutional-institutions.md)) — the two can move independently, as Research Phase 1's evidence for this document's Constitutional Principle shows directly.

## Examples from the Creative Ecosystem

- Scooter Braun's Ithaca Holdings acquiring Big Machine — a function-performing label, not a catalogue in isolation — outside any relationship with the artist whose work it held ([Artist Managers](managers.md)).
- BMG Rights Management acquiring Mute and Sanctuary as going concerns, alongside an unrelated administration-only service line for other artists within the same institution ([Publishers & Catalogue Funds](publishers.md)).

## Notes

A related but distinct pattern — personal capacity leveraged to found an entirely new institution, later itself sold (Dr. Dre / Beats Electronics) — was examined and found not to fit this document's Constitutional Principle: Beats performs none of the Constitutional Functions this methodology examines. It is tracked separately as [CQ-11](constitutional-analysis-method.md#cq-11-personal-constitutional-capacity-leveraged-into-a-new-institution), not folded in here.
