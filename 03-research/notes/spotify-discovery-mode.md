# Spotify — Discovery Mode

## Source

- [Members of Congress Slam Daniel Ek and Spotify Over 'Troubling' Discovery Mode Policy — Variety](https://variety.com/2022/music/news/congress-spotify-troubling-discovery-mode-policy-1235226722/)
- [Spotify Hit With Class Action Lawsuit Alleging Discovery Mode Is A 'Pay-For-Play Scheme' — Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/conormurray/2025/11/05/spotify-hit-with-class-action-lawsuit-alleging-discovery-mode-is-a-pay-for-play-scheme/)

## Notes

- Spotify introduced Discovery Mode in 2020: artists or their labels can boost a track's visibility in algorithmic recommendations in exchange for accepting a reduced per-stream royalty rate on that track — no cash payment, but a lower rate rather than the market rate.
- In June 2021, Representatives Jerry Nadler and Hank Johnson Jr. wrote to Spotify warning the programme could pressure artists to accept lower royalties to gain visibility; in 2022, Representatives Yvette Clarke, Judy Chu and Tony Cárdenas raised similar concerns, calling it a "serious risk for musicians" that primarily benefits the platform.
- The comparison repeatedly made, including by lawmakers, is to radio payola — historically, a human decision-maker (a DJ or programmer) accepting payment to favour a specific track, which was recognisable and regulable as a discrete human decision.
- A 2025 class-action lawsuit alleges Discovery Mode functions as a "pay-for-play scheme."
