# Artist Managers — Constitutional Participant Analysis

## Purpose

Discover the constitutional role of artist managers within the creative ecosystem, applying the [Constitutional Participant Analysis](constitutional-participant-analysis.md) methodology established in [Independent Labels — Constitutional Participant Analysis](independent-labels.md), to inform POLiPHONiC.

## Scope and Sources

Three managers, chosen for contrasting relationships to the same underlying function: Peter Grant (protector/negotiator, Led Zeppelin, 1968–1980), Coran Capshaw (business-builder, Red Light Management, Dave Matthews Band since 1991), and Scooter Braun (capital/ownership, SB Projects and Ithaca Holdings). Findings are drawn from public reporting, sourced in [03-research/notes/peter-grant.md](../03-research/notes/peter-grant.md), [03-research/notes/coran-capshaw.md](../03-research/notes/coran-capshaw.md) and [03-research/notes/scooter-braun.md](../03-research/notes/scooter-braun.md). As with [Independent Labels](independent-labels.md), this is public-record analysis, not internal operating data. No new constitutional category is asserted from these three cases alone — candidates are held in Constitutional Questions below.

## Manager Summaries

- **Peter Grant** — managed Led Zeppelin from 1968 to 1980. Negotiated a 90/10 gate-revenue split in the band's favour with concert promoters, against a prevailing industry norm of promoters keeping 40–50%, backed by personal reputation and a "take it or leave it" posture rather than institutional infrastructure.
- **Coran Capshaw** — began managing Dave Matthews Band in 1991, having first booked them at the Charlottesville venue he owned. Founded Red Light Management the same year; it grew to represent multiple artists (Phish, Chris Stapleton, The Strokes), and Capshaw became the primary initial investor in Bonnaroo, with the company later holding equity in further festivals.
- **Scooter Braun** — an artist manager (SB Projects) known for developing early-career pop artists including Justin Bieber and Ariana Grande. Never Taylor Swift's manager; his separate holding company, Ithaca Holdings, acquired her former label Big Machine (and its masters) in 2019, which Swift publicly opposed, and sold the masters on in 2020.

## Human Capacities Managers Strengthen

- **Grant**: a protective, negotiating capacity — converting personal reputation and leverage directly into economic terms on an artist's behalf, in an industry otherwise structured to extract more value from artists than from their representation.
- **Capshaw**: a capacity for turning one artist relationship into durable infrastructure that can then support other artists and ventures beyond the relationship that produced it.
- **Braun**: an early-career talent-development capacity (evident in SB Projects), distinct from — and, in the Ithaca Holdings case, disconnected from — the capital-deployment capacity that later acquired an unrelated artist's catalogue.

## Constitutional Functions They Fulfil

Negotiation and deal representation, career strategy, coordination between labels, promoters and agents, and business administration are the [Constitutional Functions](constitutional-functions.md) common to all three. Capshaw's and Braun's cases show a further function appearing once managerial capacity scales: capital deployment and asset ownership, which is not obviously the same function as representing a client.

## Constitutional Contribution

Grant's 90/10 renegotiation is [Constitutional Contribution](constitutional-contribution.md) whose effect reached beyond his own client relationship, resetting terms industry-wide. Capshaw's founding of Red Light Management and investment in festival infrastructure is contribution that outlived and outgrew the single relationship (Dave Matthews Band) that began it. Braun's acquisition of Big Machine is a harder case: because he was never Swift's manager, there was no managerial relationship for a contribution to be exercised within — the acquisition is better understood as a capital transaction adjacent to the managerial function, not an instance of it.

## Constitutional Value Created

- Grant: value that reset bargaining terms for artists generally, not only Led Zeppelin — an effect extending past his own institution's participants.
- Capshaw: value multiplied past a single artist's career length by converting personal manager capacity into a company and festival equity positions.
- Braun: a different kind of value — the market/financial value of a catalogue itself ($330m acquisition price, $405m resale) — captured through ownership rather than produced through a managerial function performed for the catalogue's creator.

## Where Constitutional Capacity Is Lost Through Administration

Grant's negotiating capacity was carried almost entirely by him personally — there was no separable institutional function absorbing the administrative and enforcement load, so his own capacity was the infrastructure. Capshaw's case shows an alternative: once a management relationship scales into a company, functions like royalty administration, logistics and legal work can be held organisationally, freeing individual managers within Red Light Management to spend more of their own capacity on artist-specific judgment — a pattern resembling Secretly Group's shared back-office model in the labels analysis. Braun's case points to a different kind of loss, not yet named here: administrative and capital capacity, once separated from the specific artist relationship it originated in, was able to act on an artist's value without her ever having engaged it. Whether this is a normal extension of the administration/creative-capacity tradeoff already observed, or a distinct category, is not settled by one case and is held as an open question below.

## Where POLiPHONiC Could Increase Constitutional Capacity

The pattern from the labels analysis recurs here: centralising the recurring, learnable [Constitutional Functions](constitutional-functions.md) — contracts, royalty tracking, tour logistics — as shared infrastructure, similar to Red Light Management relative to any single manager within it, could free managerial capacity for the specific human capacities these cases demonstrate: protective negotiation (Grant) and the judgment to translate one relationship into durable structure (Capshaw). The Braun case is a caution alongside that precedent, not a further instance of it: it suggests POLiPHONiC's design may need to keep the administrative function and any capital/ownership function structurally separate, not just the administrative and creative functions — but this distinction rests on one case and has not been tested against further evidence, so it is held as a question rather than a design conclusion.

## Notes

Findings are drawn from public reporting on three real managers, not internal operating data, and should be treated as hypotheses to weigh, not conclusions to apply directly. See Constitutional Questions below for what remains open.

## Constitutional Questions

Per the [Constitutional Analysis Method](constitutional-analysis-method.md), a category is discovered through repeated evidence across multiple participant analyses, not created from a single case. Evidence added since is tracked in the consolidated bank in [Constitutional Analysis Method](constitutional-analysis-method.md#constitutional-questions-bank), not here — this section is not revised as evidence accumulates. The following were open questions, not conclusions, as of this analysis:

1. **Should the administrative function and a capital/ownership function be treated as two distinct constitutional categories, rather than one?** The Braun/Ithaca case shows administrative capacity decoupling entirely from the artist relationship it originated in and acting on an unrelated artist's value — unlike the ordinary administration-versus-creative-capacity tradeoff seen in Grant's and Capshaw's cases, or in the labels analysis. Observed once; not yet a settled category.
2. **Does value that extends past an institution's own participants — Grant's terms benefiting artists industry-wide, not only Led Zeppelin — belong within Constitutional Value as currently scoped, or point to a broader category of externality value?** Not settled from one case.
3. **Is a manager scaling into a company with shared infrastructure (Capshaw, Red Light Management) the same constitutional pattern as a label scaling into a group (Secretly Group)?** This is now the second observed instance of that pattern. Two data points are closer to the repeated evidence this methodology requires before naming a category, but are not yet sufficient on their own.
