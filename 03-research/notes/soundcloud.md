# SoundCloud

## Source

- [SoundCloud Bankruptcy Nears: 173 People Laid Off; 2 Offices Closed — Digital Music News](https://www.digitalmusicnews.com/2017/07/06/soundcloud-layoffs-offices-closed/)
- [SoundCloud's new, game-changing payout model — SoundCloud community post](https://legacy-community.soundcloud.com/fanpoweredroyalties)

## Notes

- Founded by Alex Ljung and Eric Wahlforss as an open, upload-anything platform, distinct from curated-catalogue services.
- In July 2017, facing collapse, SoundCloud laid off 173 employees (about 40% of staff) and closed its San Francisco and London offices, as a condition of $70 million in debt funding from Ares Capital, Kreos Capital and Davidson Technology; founders said the cuts bought runway only "until Q4."
- In August 2017, a $169.5 million investment from The Raine Group and Temasek rescued the company; new leadership (CEO Kerry Trainor, COO Michael Weissman) joined.
- In 2021, SoundCloud launched Fan-Powered Royalties, a user-centric payout model that routes a portion of each subscriber's fee directly to the artists that specific subscriber actually listens to, rather than pooling all revenue and dividing it by total platform-wide stream share (the standard pro-rata model). SoundCloud reported this increased independent-creator payouts by up to roughly 25% relative to pro-rata.
