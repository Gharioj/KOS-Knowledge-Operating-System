# Constitutional Research Roadmap

## Purpose

Transform the participant analyses completed so far — [Independent Labels](independent-labels.md), [Artist Managers](managers.md) and [Producers](producers.md) — into a long-term constitutional research programme. Groups the Constitutional Questions accumulated in [Constitutional Analysis Method](constitutional-analysis-method.md#constitutional-questions-bank) into themes, and sequences future participant analyses to maximise constitutional learning, per [Constitutional Analysis Method](constitutional-analysis-method.md).

## Constitutional Themes

The seven banked questions, as of this roadmap, cluster into four themes. Grouping into a theme is a way of reading the bank, not a change to it — no question's own state or evidence entry is altered here.

### Theme A: Institutional Scaling & Shared Infrastructure

**CQ-2, CQ-6, CQ-7.** Whether centralising administrative or methodological infrastructure while separating creative judgment per unit is one recurring constitutional pattern, and whether it is a good structural precedent for POLiPHONiC. The most evidence-rich theme so far — three adjacent-but-not-identical framings, each with 1–2 data points (Secretly Group, Red Light Management, Max Martin's Cheiron→Maratone→MXM lineage), none yet sharpened into the single falsifiable claim resolution would require.

### Theme B: Capacity-to-Capital Conversion

**CQ-4.** Whether administrative capacity and capital/ownership capacity are distinct constitutional categories. Two data points so far (Scooter Braun/Ithaca Holdings, Dr. Dre/Beats Electronics), both from participant types whose core function is something else (managing, producing) and whose capital conversion was a secondary development, not their defining activity. A participant type whose core function *is* capital/rights ownership has not yet been analysed.

### Theme C: Boundary Conditions of Recognition & Value

**CQ-1, CQ-5.** Both stretch the edges of existing definitions: CQ-1 asks whether an institution's public self-correction counts as Recognised Contribution beyond the mere absence of further harm; CQ-5 asks whether value extending past an institution's own participants belongs within Constitutional Value as currently scoped. Both are single-instance and under-evidenced relative to Theme A.

### Theme D: Methodological Sufficiency (cross-cutting)

**CQ-3.** Whether public-reporting-only analysis is sufficient grounding. This recurs automatically with every analysis regardless of participant type — it is not something a specific participant choice targets, and is tracked separately from the sequencing recommendations below.

## Candidate Participant Groups

Four candidates, chosen for how directly they bear on the themes above rather than for topical interest alone. None have been analysed yet — the discoveries listed are hypotheses motivating the choice, not conclusions; per [Constitutional Analysis Method](constitutional-analysis-method.md), only an actual completed analysis can produce evidence.

### 1. Publishers & Catalogue Funds

Music publishers and rights/catalogue acquisition vehicles (e.g. traditional publishers, or funds such as Hipgnosis Songs Fund) whose core function *is* owning and administering rights, unlike Braun or Dre for whom capital conversion was secondary to managing or producing.

- **Existing questions it may help resolve:** CQ-4 most directly — a third, differently-shaped instance where capital/ownership is the primary function rather than a secondary development, and from a participant type distinct from both prior instances. Also relevant to Theme A, since publishers commonly administer many songwriters' catalogues under one shared infrastructure.
- **New questions likely to emerge:** whether a participant whose entire function is capital/ownership can meaningfully be analysed for Human Capacities at all, or whether the methodology needs a variant for capital-native institutions.
- **Expected constitutional discoveries (hypothesis):** the clearest test yet of whether Theme B is a real constitutional category or an artefact of two coincidentally similar cases.

### 2. Session Musicians & Sidemen

Individual contributors whose capacity is routinely exercised within a recording but not always credited or recognised — already named as an example in [Constitutional Capacity](constitutional-capacity.md) and [Constitutional Contribution](constitutional-contribution.md) before any participant analysis existed.

- **Existing questions it may help resolve:** CQ-1 directly (a further, individual-level instance of the recognition gap, contrasting with OLI's institutional-level self-correction) — and, more broadly, tests the Human Capacities → Constitutional Capacity gap at the individual level, which all three completed analyses examined only at the institutional/founder level.
- **New questions likely to emerge:** whether the constitutional analysis method, built so far around institutions and their founders, applies unchanged to individual, non-founding contributors, or needs adaptation.
- **Expected constitutional discoveries (hypothesis):** the sharpest available test of the capacity/recognition gap the methodology already flags as its central concern.

### 3. Booking Agents & Touring Agencies

Agencies representing artists for live performance (e.g. CAA, WME, Paradigm), structurally similar to management companies but organised around a different constitutional function.

- **Existing questions it may help resolve:** Theme A (CQ-2, CQ-6, CQ-7) most directly — a fourth data point on shared-infrastructure scaling, from a participant type adjacent to but distinct from labels, managers and producers, which could help determine whether the theme's three current framings are one pattern or several.
- **New questions likely to emerge:** how the touring/live function relates to the recording-centred functions all three completed analyses have examined.
- **Expected constitutional discoveries (hypothesis):** likely to consolidate Theme A rather than open new territory — useful, but lower-novelty than Publishers or Session Musicians.

### 4. Streaming Platforms & DSPs

Digital service providers (e.g. streaming platforms) — infrastructure/technology institutions rather than institutions built around a human creative capacity in the way labels, managers and producers are.

- **Existing questions it may help resolve:** potentially CQ-5 (externality value), since platform effects plausibly extend far beyond any one institution's own participants.
- **New questions likely to emerge:** whether the methodology's core vocabulary — Human Capacities, Constitutional Functions — meaningfully applies to a technology-infrastructure participant at all, rather than a participant type that requires different foundational concepts.
- **Expected constitutional discoveries (hypothesis):** the highest-risk, most speculative candidate — valuable for stress-testing the methodology's limits, but only once it is better established from clearer cases.

## Recommended Sequence

1. **Publishers & Catalogue Funds** — highest expected constitutional value: the sharpest available test of Theme B (CQ-4), from a participant type not yet examined.
2. **Session Musicians & Sidemen** — independent axis (individual rather than institutional capacity), directly tests CQ-1 and the capacity/recognition gap the methodology has flagged as central since its first document.
3. **Booking Agents & Touring Agencies** — consolidates Theme A with a fourth data point; valuable, but more confirmatory than novel given three framings of that theme already exist.
4. **Streaming Platforms & DSPs** — highest risk and most speculative fit with the methodology's current vocabulary; best attempted once more conventional participant types have further tested the method's stability.

## Dependencies

- No candidate strictly blocks another — all four could be analysed in any order without breaking the method.
- **Session Musicians** is fully independent of the other three; it could run first, second, or in parallel, and is placed second here for balance between institutional and individual axes, not because it depends on Publishers.
- **Streaming Platforms** has a soft dependency on Publishers being analysed first: understanding how Constitutional Value and Contribution behave at the rights-administration layer is likely to make an analysis of the downstream distribution layer more stable. This is a sequencing preference, not a hard requirement.
- **Booking Agents** has no dependency but is more valuable after at least one more Theme A data point exists from a non-institutional-scaling-focused analysis (Publishers or Session Musicians), so its consolidating role is judged against a wider base of evidence.

## Notes

This roadmap is a recommendation, not a decision — the human may reorder, skip, or add participant groups freely; nothing here binds a future Constitutional Intent. Expected discoveries listed above are hypotheses that justified the sequencing choice, not findings; only a completed analysis, per [Constitutional Analysis Method](constitutional-analysis-method.md), produces evidence. This roadmap itself should be revisited once any of the four candidates is analysed, since its sequencing logic depends on the current, still-thin state of the Questions Bank.
