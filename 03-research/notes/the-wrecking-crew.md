# The Wrecking Crew

## Source

- [The Wrecking Crew (music) — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Wrecking_Crew_(music))
- [Carol Kaye vs. Hal Blaine feud, and 2025 Rock Hall induction controversy — Variety](https://variety.com/2025/music/news/carol-kaye-danny-tedesco-respoinds-wrecking-crew-rock-hall-1236437946/)

## Notes

- An informal group of Los Angeles session musicians (Hal Blaine, Carol Kaye, Tommy Tedesco and others) who played on hundreds of hit recordings in the 1960s–70s for the Beach Boys, the Byrds, the Monkees, Sonny & Cher, the Mamas and the Papas and many more, without an official name or credit at the time.
- The "Wrecking Crew" name was popularised later by Hal Blaine's 1990 memoir; Carol Kaye disputes that the name was used contemporaneously and has objected to it publicly.
- Recognition arrived decades later and through external mediation: Blaine and session player Earl Palmer were inaugural "sidemen" inductees to the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2000; the group was inducted into the Musicians Hall of Fame in 2007; a documentary (completed 2008, released 2015 after a Kickstarter-funded licensing campaign) brought wider public recognition; the group was inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame's Musical Excellence category in 2025.
- In 2025, Carol Kaye publicly declined her Rock Hall induction and boycotted the ceremony, stating that singling out individual players goes against the collaborative spirit of the group's work, and criticising the Hall's continued use of the "Wrecking Crew" name.

## Relevance

Grounds the "Recognised Contribution & Attribution" and "Constitutional Questions" sections of [07-constitutional-methodology/session-musicians.md](../../07-constitutional-methodology/session-musicians.md): a canonical case of contribution recognised only decades after the fact, through institutions other than the one that engaged the musicians — and a rare, concrete instance of offered recognition being publicly declined, on stated grounds.
