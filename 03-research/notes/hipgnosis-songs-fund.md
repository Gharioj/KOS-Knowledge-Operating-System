## Notes

- Founded in 2018 by Merck Mercuriadis as a publicly listed fund whose entire function was acquiring music catalogues — not administering catalogues on behalf of writers who retain ownership, but buying the rights outright.
- Spent upward of $2 billion acquiring catalogues from songwriters and artists including Neil Young, Shakira, Lindsey Buckingham, Journey, Blondie and others' royalty shares from songs performed by Justin Bieber, among many more.
- Blackstone took a $1 billion stake in the fund's investment adviser in 2021, then acquired the publicly listed fund outright for $1.584 billion in 2024, following a competing bid from Concord.
- After acquisition, Hipgnosis Songs Fund's ~40,000 songs were combined with privately held Hipgnosis Songs Capital and the investment adviser, and the combined, Blackstone-owned company was rebranded Recognition Music Group. Mercuriadis stepped down as chairman.
