## Notes

- Founded in London, 1985, by Derek Birkett (former Flux of Pink Indians bassist), Sue Birkett and Tim Kelly, as One Little Indian Records.
- Early roster included The Sugarcubes — featuring Björk — and other acts from the Crass Records circle; became best known for its decades-long relationship with Björk as a solo artist.
- In June 2020, following worldwide protests after the murder of George Floyd, and after receiving a letter from a fan explaining the offensive history of the "Indian" name, the label renamed itself One Little Independent Records with immediate effect and committed to donating to organisations supporting Native American communities.
- Birkett publicly acknowledged the harm of the original name rather than defending institutional continuity.
