# Peter Grant

## Source

- [Peter Grant (music manager) — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Grant_(music_manager))
- [How 'Rock's Greatest Manager' Shaped Led Zeppelin's Success — Here & Now, WBUR](https://www.wbur.org/hereandnow/2018/11/26/led-zeppelin-peter-grant-manager)

## Notes

- Managed Led Zeppelin from their formation in 1968 until the band's breakup in 1980, after earlier work as a tour manager for acts including Chuck Berry and the Animals.
- Negotiated a 90/10 split of ticket gate revenue in the band's favour with concert promoters, at a time when promoters typically kept 40–50% — an unprecedented reversal of the standard split.
- Achieved this through a "take it or leave it" negotiating posture backed by his physical presence and reputation, rather than through a separate legal or administrative apparatus.
- Widely credited with shifting bargaining power in the touring industry from promoters and agents toward artists and their management generally, not only for Led Zeppelin.
