# Independent Labels — Constitutional Participant Analysis

## Purpose

Apply the constitutional methodology to three independent record labels — Mute Records, One Little Independent Records, and Secretly Group — to discover enduring constitutional principles capable of informing POLiPHONiC. This is a [Constitutional Participant Analysis](constitutional-participant-analysis.md), carried out ahead of [Constitutional Analysis Method](constitutional-analysis-method.md) itself being developed — it is a first concrete exercise of that method, not yet a validation of a written procedure.

## Scope and Sources

Three institutions, chosen for contrasting structures around a similar core: a single-founder model (Mute), a founder-and-artist model (One Little Independent), and a founder-partnership, shared-infrastructure model (Secretly Group). Findings are drawn from public reporting, sourced in [03-research/notes/mute-records.md](../03-research/notes/mute-records.md), [03-research/notes/one-little-independent-records.md](../03-research/notes/one-little-independent-records.md) and [03-research/notes/secretly-group.md](../03-research/notes/secretly-group.md). This is public-record analysis, not access to internal operating data — its conclusions are hypotheses to test against POLiPHONiC's actual conditions, not settled fact.

## Institution Summaries

- **Mute Records** (London, 1978) — founded and, for most of its history, singularly directed by Daniel Miller. Built its identity on his own A&R judgment across Depeche Mode, Yazoo, Erasure, Nick Cave and the Bad Seeds, Moby and Goldfrapp. Sold to EMI in 2002, taken independent again in 2010; its back catalogue was acquired by BMG in 2012 for a sum the acquirer attributed explicitly to Miller's "vision and A&R skills."
- **One Little Independent Records** (London, 1985, as One Little Indian) — founded by Derek Birkett, Sue Birkett and Tim Kelly, best known for its multi-decade relationship with Björk. In 2020, following the murder of George Floyd and a fan's letter explaining the harm of the original name, the label publicly renamed itself and committed funds to Native American organisations.
- **Secretly Group** (Bloomington, Indiana) — Secretly Canadian (1996), joined by Jagjaguwar (1999) and Dead Oceans (2007), later the Numero Group (2013) and a 50% stake in Merge Records. Multiple founder-partners, each running a distinct imprint with its own aesthetic identity, sharing offices and staff.

## Human Capacities Each Institution Nurtures

- **Mute**: a singular, sustained A&R capacity — the ability to recognise and shape an artist's identity across an entire career, not just a release cycle.
- **One Little Independent**: a protective, long-horizon relational capacity — the ability to hold a creative partnership steady over decades, and, distinctly, the capacity for institutional self-correction when a past choice is recognised as harmful.
- **Secretly Group**: a distributed creative capacity held by several founder-partners at once, each responsible for a separate aesthetic identity, combined with a shared organisational capacity (offices, staff) that none of the individual imprints has to hold alone.

## Constitutional Functions They Fulfil

All three perform the same core [Constitutional Functions](constitutional-functions.md) — A&R, catalogue stewardship, artist career management, rights and royalty administration, promotion — but organise them differently. Mute and One Little Independent concentrate these functions around one founder's judgment; Secretly Group separates the creative functions (A&R, aesthetic direction) per imprint while centralising the administrative functions (offices, staff, presumably financial and rights administration) across all of them.

## Constitutional Contribution

Observed [Constitutional Contribution](constitutional-contribution.md) in each case: Mute's decades of catalogue-building now recognised as having independent acquisition value; One Little Independent's sustained stewardship of Björk's career, and its 2020 renaming — itself a contribution, since correcting a harmful name is an act, not merely an omission corrected; Secretly Group's shared infrastructure, which is what let three (later five) distinct creative identities each survive without each having to separately build the same back-office capability.

## Constitutional Value Created

- Mute: durable catalogue value that persisted past any single artist's active career, evidenced by BMG paying to acquire it in 2012 — a decade after Miller himself had already taken the label independent again.
- One Little Independent: reputational and trust value built from a single long artist relationship, and — after 2020 — value attached to the label's willingness to correct itself publicly rather than protect its existing brand.
- Secretly Group: portfolio resilience value — creative risk is spread across several distinct imprints while administrative cost is shared, so no single imprint's commercial fortune determines the group's survival.

## Which Contributions Should Become Recognised Contributions

The clearest gap across all three cases is the same one identified in [Constitutional Capacity](constitutional-capacity.md): the contribution with the most enduring value — one person's sustained A&R judgment (Mute), one person's protective long-term relationship management (One Little Independent) — is the least formally accounted for. BMG's 2012 valuation of the Mute catalogue is the closest thing to an external, monetary recognition of Daniel Miller's contribution, and it arrived decades after the contribution itself. The staff who sustain Secretly Group's shared back-office function are structurally essential to every imprint's survival but are not visible in any of the public material reviewed. One Little Independent's 2020 renaming is a single observed instance of an institution revising a past decision once its harm was recognised — whether that constitutes its own category of [Recognised Contribution](recognised-contribution.md), distinct from the absence of further harm, is addressed as an open question below rather than concluded from one case.

## Where POLiPHONiC Could Increase Constitutional Capacity

Across all three institutions, the pattern is the same: constitutional value concentrates wherever a small number of people are able to sustain deep creative judgment over long periods, and it is put at risk wherever that judgment is diluted by administrative load. Secretly Group's shared-infrastructure model is one observed way of separating the two: centralising the recurring, learnable [Constitutional Functions](constitutional-functions.md) — rights administration, financial administration, catalogue and metadata management — so that founders and A&R judgment are spent on the [Human Capacities](human-capacities.md) that cannot be centralised: taste, long-term relationship, and the willingness to self-correct in public. Whether this particular pattern, rather than the single-founder model of Mute or One Little Independent, is the right structural precedent for POLiPHONiC is addressed as an open question below.

## Notes

Findings are drawn from public reporting on three real institutions, not from internal operating data, and should be treated as hypotheses to weigh, not conclusions to apply directly. Whether and how any of this should shape POLiPHONiC's actual design is a judgment call for the human, not a conclusion this document can reach on its own — see Constitutional Questions below for what specifically remains open.

## Constitutional Questions

These remain open. Per the constitutional methodology's own discipline, a category is discovered through repeated evidence accumulated across multiple participant analyses — it is not created from a single case. Until further participant analyses exist, the following are questions, not conclusions:

1. **Is institutional self-correction its own category of Recognised Contribution?** One Little Independent's 2020 renaming is one observed instance of an institution revising a past decision once its harm was recognised. Whether this is a distinct, creditable category of [Recognised Contribution](recognised-contribution.md) — rather than simply the absence of further harm — cannot be settled from this single case.
2. **Is Secretly Group's structure the right model for POLiPHONiC?** Centralising administrative [Constitutional Functions](constitutional-functions.md) while separating creative judgment per imprint is one structural pattern, observed once, alongside two contrasting single-founder models (Mute, One Little Independent). Whether it is the right model for POLiPHONiC, rather than merely a viable one among several, is not established here.
3. **Is public-reporting-only analysis sufficient grounding?** All three institutions were analysed from public reporting, with no internal operating data. Whether this is sufficient grounding to act on, or whether primary research is required before these findings inform POLiPHONiC's design, is unresolved.
