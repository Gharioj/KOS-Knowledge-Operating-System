# Constitutional Review — Research Phase 1

## Purpose

A concise constitutional review of Research Phase 1 — the seven completed participant analyses ([Independent Labels](independent-labels.md), [Artist Managers](managers.md), [Producers](producers.md), [Publishers & Catalogue Funds](publishers.md), [Session Musicians & Sidemen](session-musicians.md), [Booking Agents & Touring Agencies](booking-agents.md), [Streaming Platforms & DSPs](streaming-platforms.md)) and the resulting [Constitutional Questions Bank](constitutional-analysis-method.md#constitutional-questions-bank). Assesses which questions have reached the evidentiary maturity [Constitutional Analysis Method](constitutional-analysis-method.md) requires for resolution. This review does not resolve any question and does not create any Constitutional Principle — resolution remains a distinct, human-reviewed act.

## Research Phase 1 Achievements

- Seven participant analyses completed, spanning three structurally different kinds of participant: institutions with founders (labels, managers, producers, publishers, booking agencies), an individual, project-by-project participant type (session musicians), and an infrastructure-native, non-human-capacity participant type (streaming platforms).
- Ten Constitutional Questions banked (CQ-1–CQ-10), each with a permanent, append-only evidence trail and an explicit state — none deleted, none prematurely resolved.
- The Constitutional Resolution state model and its four-part readiness criteria (independent-group count, cross-type spread, absence of a disconfirming instance, a single falsifiable claim) were defined after the Independent Labels correction, then tested against real accumulated evidence for the first time in this review.
- The methodology's core vocabulary (Human Capacities, Constitutional Functions) was tested against its most divergent case, Streaming Platforms, and held — revealing that Human Capacity can be displaced (exercised once, upstream, at design time) rather than exercised directly at the point a function runs.
- 21 sourced research notes now ground every claim made across the seven analyses, in [03-research/notes/](../03-research/README.md).

## Questions Ready for Constitutional Review

**CQ-4** (administration versus capital/ownership as distinct constitutional categories) is the only question meeting three of the four resolution criteria: 5 independent evidentiary groups (exceeds the 3-group minimum), spanning five different participant types (exceeds the 2-type minimum), and no evidence across any completed analysis actually contradicts the underlying claim. It fails the fourth criterion: the claim has never been reduced to one precise, falsifiable statement that all five pieces of evidence satisfy. Each new instance — Braun, Dre, Hipgnosis and BMG, Wasserman/Paradigm, Bandcamp — has complicated the boundary between administration and capital rather than sharpening it.

CQ-4 is therefore ready for a human Constitutional Review session, but that session's most likely first task is sharpening the claim itself, not resolving it outright. "Ready for review" and "ready to resolve" are not the same thing here.

No other individual question meets both the count and cross-type thresholds. The next closest are CQ-1, CQ-2, CQ-5 and CQ-6, each at 2 evidentiary groups.

## A Second Candidate, Conditional on a Prior Decision

CQ-2, CQ-6 and CQ-7 are three related-but-distinct framings of what may be a single shared-infrastructure pattern, raised independently across three analyses (Independent Labels, Artist Managers, Producers) and reinforced by a fourth (Booking Agents), with four combined evidentiary instances in total: Secretly Group, Red Light Management, Max Martin's Cheiron→Maratone→MXM lineage, and CAA. No individual question in this cluster meets the 3-group threshold alone.

If a human review determines these are genuinely the same question — a Merged event, per [Constitutional Analysis Method](constitutional-analysis-method.md) — the resulting single question would very plausibly meet the count and cross-type criteria immediately, making it the bank's second question ready for review. This review does not make that determination; it flags the cluster as the second priority for human attention, contingent on the merge decision itself coming first.

## Questions Requiring Further Evidence

- **CQ-1** (institutional self-correction) — 2 groups, differently shaped (a discrete institutional decision versus a blanket policy reversal plus third-party mediation); needs a third instance, and arguably needs its current two reconciled before a third would sharpen rather than further broaden it.
- **CQ-2, CQ-6, CQ-7** — see above: individually under-evidenced, collectively substantial, gated on a merge decision.
- **CQ-3** (sufficiency of public-reporting-only analysis) — technically at 3 groups, but this is a standing methodological condition present in every analysis by default, not an independently discovered pattern in the same sense as the others. Whether it should be assessed against the same resolution criteria at all, or handled differently, is itself unresolved — see Recommended Priorities below.
- **CQ-5** (externality value beyond an institution's own participants) — 2 groups, via two different mechanisms (renegotiating existing terms versus building new structure); a third instance would test whether the mechanism matters to the claim.
- **CQ-8** (capital-native value durability), **CQ-9** (compensation/recognition decoupling), **CQ-10** (automated execution of a Constitutional Function) — each 1 group; genuinely new territory raised across the last three analyses, too early to assess.

## Recommended Priorities for Research Phase 2

1. **Human review of CQ-4** — the highest-priority item. Not a resolution decision yet, but a claim-sharpening session: evidentiary accumulation alone has not converged on one precise statement across five analyses, and further participant analyses are unlikely to sharpen it further without that session happening first.
2. **Human review of the Theme A cluster (CQ-2/CQ-6/CQ-7)** for a possible Merged decision — second priority, since it could unlock the bank's second-most-mature question.
3. **A fresh Phase 2 participant sequence.** The Research Roadmap's four recommended groups are exhausted, and no further participant group is currently sequenced (per the [Constitutional Programme Dashboard](constitutional-programme-dashboard.md)). A new prioritisation — most plausibly targeting a third instance for CQ-1 or CQ-5, or a cross-type test for the Theme A cluster once any merge decision is made — is needed before another participant analysis proceeds.
4. **A decision on CQ-3's standing** — whether a methodological-sufficiency question like this should ever be assessed against the same resolution criteria as a substantive constitutional claim, or should be handled as a different, standing category entirely.
