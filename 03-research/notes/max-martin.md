## Notes

- Signed to Denniz Pop's Cheiron Studios (a BMG-affiliated label/production house in Sweden); Pop recognised Martin's songwriting talent and mentored him.
- Cheiron produced early Backstreet Boys and Britney Spears hits, including Spears's "…Baby One More Time" (1998), Martin's first US number one.
- After Denniz Pop died of cancer in 1998, Martin took over as Cheiron's director; the studio closed in 2000.
- Martin and Tom Talomaa founded Maratone in 2001; Martin later co-founded MXM with Shellback (Johan Schuster) in 2011, continuing a broadly similar production methodology under successive company names.
- Holds 26 Billboard Hot 100 number-one hits as producer — the most of any producer in the chart's history — across artists including Britney Spears, Katy Perry, Taylor Swift and Ariana Grande.
