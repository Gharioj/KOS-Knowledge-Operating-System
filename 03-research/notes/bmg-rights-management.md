# BMG Rights Management

## Source

- [BMG Rights Management — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BMG_Rights_Management)
- [Music as an Investment – Part 2: BMG re-enters the music rights business — Music Business Research](https://musicbusinessresearch.wordpress.com/2023/04/04/music-as-an-investment-part-2-bmg-re-enters-the-music-rights-business/)

## Notes

- Launched by Bertelsmann in October 2008 with around 200 artists' rights retained from Sony BMG, amid the 2008 financial crisis; a 51% stake was sold to KKR in 2009 for funding.
- Operates an "Artist Services" model under which the artist keeps ownership of their copyrights and BMG provides funding, royalty administration, accounting and synch services — structurally similar to Kobalt's administration-only model.
- Separately and simultaneously acquires whole catalogues outright: Mute (2012, also covered in [independent-labels.md](../../07-constitutional-methodology/independent-labels.md)), Sanctuary (2013), and the EMI Virgin Music and Famous Music UK publishing catalogues from Sony/ATV — full ownership acquisitions, not administration-only relationships.
- Represents rights to over one million songs and recordings across eight core markets, combining both models within one institution.

## Relevance

Grounds the "Constitutional Functions" and "Where Constitutional Capacity Is Lost Through Administration" sections of [07-constitutional-methodology/publishers.md](../../07-constitutional-methodology/publishers.md): a single institution running both an administration-only model (like Kobalt) and a capital/ownership-acquisition model (like Hipgnosis) side by side, complicating a clean administration-versus-capital categorisation rather than confirming it.
