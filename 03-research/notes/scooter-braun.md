## Notes

- Braun is an artist manager (SB Projects), known for developing early-career pop artists including Justin Bieber and Ariana Grande.
- Braun was never Taylor Swift's manager. In June 2019, his separate holding company, Ithaca Holdings, acquired Big Machine Label Group — Swift's former label — for a reported $330m, which included Big Machine's masters, publishing rights and distribution deals.
- Swift publicly objected, stating she learned of the acquisition when it was announced, and described it as her "worst case scenario" after years of asking to own her own masters.
- In October 2020, Braun sold the masters to Shamrock Holdings for a reported $405m. Swift began re-recording her back catalogue rather than continue under the new ownership.

## Relevance

Grounds the "Constitutional Contribution" and "Where Constitutional Capacity Is Lost Through Administration" sections of [07-constitutional-methodology/managers.md](../../07-constitutional-methodology/managers.md): a case where capital/administrative capacity, built up through management work, was deployed to acquire a catalogue outside any managerial relationship with its creator — a decoupling of administrative and ownership capacity from the artist relationship such capacity is nominally meant to serve.
