# Nathan East

## Source

- [Nathan East — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nathan_East)
- [Nathan East on Playing Bass with Absolutely Everyone — Berklee Online](https://online.berklee.edu/takenote/bassist-nathan-east-on-playing-bass-with-clapton-beyonce-more/)

## Notes

- One of the most recorded bass players in music history, with more than 2,000 recordings across four decades, starting at sixteen touring with Barry White.
- Session and touring credits include Eric Clapton ("Tears in Heaven"), Michael Jackson ("Bad"), Whitney Houston ("Greatest Love of All"), Beyoncé ("Listen"), George Harrison, Phil Collins, Barbra Streisand and Stevie Wonder, among many others.
- Long-running member of Eric Clapton's studio and touring bands since the 1980s; honoured by the United States Congress with a Congressional Record in 2007 for his contribution to the music community — recognition granted during his active career, not posthumously or after decades of anonymity.
- Founding member of the contemporary jazz group Fourplay (with Bob James, Lee Ritenour and Harvey Mason), converting sustained session work into his own credited, ongoing musical project rather than remaining purely a hired player.

## Relevance

Grounds the "Constitutional Capacity" section of [07-constitutional-methodology/session-musicians.md](../../07-constitutional-methodology/session-musicians.md): a contrasting case where project-by-project engagement accumulated, through sheer volume and visibility, into durable individual recognition and even a self-founded institution (Fourplay) — unlike the Wrecking Crew and Funk Brothers, whose recognition arrived late and through external mediation.
