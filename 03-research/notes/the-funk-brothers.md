# The Funk Brothers

## Source

- [The Funk Brothers — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Funk_Brothers)

## Notes

- Uncredited house session musicians for Motown Records from 1959 until the label's relocation to Los Angeles in 1972, providing the instrumental foundation for the "Sound of Young America" across the label's roster.
- Motown did not credit its studio musicians on releases until Marvin Gaye's *What's Going On* in 1971 — a blanket policy covering all musicians and releases, not a decision made about any individual contributor.
- Bassist James Jamerson was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2000, drummer Benny Benjamin in 2003 — individual honours for two sidemen, decades after the recordings.
- Broader recognition came later still: Musicians Hall of Fame induction (2007), a Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award (2004) and a Hollywood Walk of Fame star (2013), driven substantially by Allan Slutsky's book and the 2002 documentary *Standing in the Shadows of Motown*, which named 13 members.
