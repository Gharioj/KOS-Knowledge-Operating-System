# Recognised Contribution

## Definition

Recognised Contribution is a [Constitutional Contribution](constitutional-contribution.md) that the system has formally acknowledged — credited, recorded, or accounted for — making it visible enough to be assigned [Constitutional Value](constitutional-value.md) and, where due, compensated or celebrated. It is not the contribution itself: contribution is real the moment it happens, independent of whether it is ever recognised.

## Constitutional Principle

Recognition is an institutional act, not an automatic consequence of contribution. It can be withheld as a matter of routine administrative policy rather than individual judgment, and — when finally offered — is not itself neutral to accept: an institution's terms for granting recognition can be contested by the person it names. A constitutional methodology is answerable for how, when and on what terms recognition is extended, not only for whether it is extended at all.

## Why Recognition Needs Its Own Accounting

Contribution and recognition can be structurally decoupled — administrative mediation can guarantee payment for a contribution while withholding recognition entirely, as a matter of routine policy rather than any decision about the specific person ([Constitutional Contribution](constitutional-contribution.md); [CQ-9](constitutional-analysis-method.md#cq-9-is-recognised-contribution-structurally-decoupled-from-compensation)). Because the two can move independently, tracking recognition only as a byproduct of tracking contribution or payment is not enough — it has to be assessed in its own right.

## Relationship to Constitutional Contribution

Recognition is applied to [Constitutional Contribution](constitutional-contribution.md), never the reverse: a contribution does not depend on recognition to be real, but recognition has nothing to attach to without a contribution having already occurred.

## Relationship to Constitutional Value

Recognition is the gate through which a contribution becomes eligible for [Constitutional Value](constitutional-value.md) — value cannot be assigned to a contribution the system has not yet acknowledged, though the value ultimately assigned may be realised much later, and by institutions other than the one the contribution was made within.

## Examples from the Creative Ecosystem

- One Little Independent's 2020 renaming — an institution publicly recognising a past harm and correcting it, itself a candidate instance of recognition as an act, not only a record ([Independent Labels](independent-labels.md); [CQ-1](constitutional-analysis-method.md#cq-1-institutional-self-correction-as-recognised-contribution)).
- Motown's 1971 reversal of its blanket policy of not crediting session musicians, followed decades later by Hall of Fame inductions and documentaries recognising the Wrecking Crew and the Funk Brothers — recognition arriving through institutions other than the one that had engaged them ([Session Musicians & Sidemen](session-musicians.md)).
- Carol Kaye's 2025 declined Rock Hall induction — recognition offered is not necessarily recognition accepted; the terms on which recognition is granted can themselves be contested by the person it names ([Session Musicians & Sidemen](session-musicians.md)).
- BMG's 2012 acquisition of Mute's catalogue, the closest thing to an external, monetary recognition of Daniel Miller's A&R judgment, arriving a decade after the contribution itself ([Independent Labels](independent-labels.md)).
