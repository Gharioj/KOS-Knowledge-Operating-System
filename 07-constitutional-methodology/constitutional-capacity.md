# Constitutional Capacity

## Definition

Constitutional Capacity is a [Human Capacity](human-capacities.md) that the system has formally recognised as belonging within the constitutional order — the point at which an ability is named, given standing, and made eligible to be organised into [Constitutional Functions](constitutional-functions.md), exercised through [Constitutional Institutions](constitutional-institutions.md), and evaluated as [Constitutional Contribution](constitutional-contribution.md). It is not the ability itself; it is the ability once recognised.

## Constitutional Principle

Recognition is a constitutional act, not a natural fact. A Human Capacity does not become a Constitutional Capacity simply by existing or being exercised — it becomes one only when the system names it and gives it standing. Recognition can therefore be withheld, delayed or unevenly applied, and a constitutional methodology is answerable for how fairly it extends recognition across people whose underlying capacities are equivalent.

## Why the Gap Between Capacity and Recognition Matters

Two people can hold the same underlying ability — the same ear, the same negotiating skill — and receive entirely different constitutional standing, because one has been recognised through a function or institution and the other has not. This gap is not incidental; it is where most constitutional failure occurs. A system that recognises capacity by proximity, credential or gatekeeping rather than by the capacity itself produces institutions built on partial foundations, however capable their participants actually are.

## Relationship to Human Capacities

Constitutional Capacity is grounded in [Human Capacity](human-capacities.md) — recognition cannot manufacture an ability that is not there. But not every Human Capacity that exists is recognised: possessing an ability and holding constitutional standing for it are two different states, and recognition is the step between them.

## Relationship to Constitutional Contribution

Once a Human Capacity is recognised as Constitutional Capacity, it becomes eligible to be exercised and counted as [Constitutional Contribution](constitutional-contribution.md). Recognition is the gate; contribution is what passes through it once the capacity is actually put to use.

## Examples from the Creative Ecosystem

- A demo from an unsigned artist carries real human capacity, but becomes constitutional capacity only once an A&R function recognises it.
- An engineer's mixing ability is recognised capacity within the institution that employs them, but may go unrecognised elsewhere until credited or referenced independently.
- A session musician's or uncredited co-writer's capacity is often exercised without the recognition step being granted at all — the clearest case of the gap this document describes, now directly evidenced by [Session Musicians & Sidemen](session-musicians.md): the Wrecking Crew and the Funk Brothers were, for decades, excluded from recognition by blanket institutional policy rather than any individual decision, then recognised only much later, and largely through institutions other than the ones that had engaged them.

## Relationship to Technology

Technology changes who or what performs the recognition step — algorithmic discovery and streaming-data-driven scouting can widen or narrow recognition faster than human gatekeepers, and can make raw capacity more visible before any formal recognition occurs. Neither reduces recognition to a mechanical fact: however it is performed, granting constitutional standing remains a decision the system is accountable for, not a byproduct of the tooling used to make it.

## Notes

The gap between Human Capacity and Constitutional Capacity is the primary site of fairness questions in this methodology — see [Constitutional Participant Analysis](constitutional-participant-analysis.md), now evidenced across seven completed Research Phase 1 analyses, for how recognition is assessed across participants, and the [Constitutional Questions Bank](constitutional-analysis-method.md#constitutional-questions-bank) (particularly CQ-1 and CQ-9) for what remains open about it.
